The Tata Motors Finance Turnaround: A Masterclass in Operational Excellence

Dear readers, this is where the story gets really interesting. On May 8, 2025, something significant happened: Tata Motors Finance Limited (which previously operated under Tata Motors) was merged into Tata Capital prior to its IPO.

Now, this business unit was actually performing poorly—it was a drag on performance. But here's where Tata Capital's management demonstrated their operational brilliance.

The Impressive Turnaround Strategy

Investors, watch how quickly they executed this turnaround:

Diversification of OEM Portfolio: Instead of focusing solely on Tata Motors vehicles, management immediately began directing attention toward other Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs). The results? In just nine months—between Q4 FY25 and the latest quarter—they increased the contribution from 'Other OEMs' from 0% to 19%. Additionally, they onboarded over 100 new dealers during this short timeframe.

Cost Optimization: Tata Motors Finance had an excessive employee base of over 6,300 staff members, which resulted in significantly high operational costs. Management took decisive action, reducing the headcount to 5,200 employees while simultaneously strengthening their credit underwriting mechanism.

This, readers, is what efficient management looks like in action.

Aggressive Branch Expansion: Building Infrastructure for Future Growth

Readers, here's another aspect that caught my attention: their branch network expansion.

By the end of the 2023 financial year, Tata Capital had established a network of over 500 branches. Fast forward to the quarter ending in March, and they've expanded this network to over 1,500 branches.

That's a 3x expansion in branch infrastructure! When you see this kind of physical presence expansion, investors, it automatically translates into higher sales figures and deeper market penetration.

This aggressive expansion demonstrates management's confidence in future growth opportunities. Each new branch represents a touchpoint for customer acquisition, whether it's for home loans, loans against property, vehicle financing, or their wealth management services.

But they're not stopping at just physical expansion. Management reports that they're making substantial investments in technology adoption initiatives as well. This combination of physical presence and technological capability positions them perfectly for the digital-first era we're entering.
